About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Chemistry at California Institute of Technology (Caltech), working in the groups of Prof. K. N. Houk and Prof. W. A. Goddard III. My research combines DFT-level computation with wet-lab synthesis to elucidate catalytic mechanisms, design new catalysts, and build predictive models for selectivity and reactivity.

Before Caltech, I earned my B.Sc. in Chemistry summa cum laude from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), where I was awarded the Guo Moruo Scholarship — the highest honor for graduating students (ranked 33 out of 1,860). I have also interned at Northwestern University (Marks Group), SUSTech (Liu Group), and UCLA (Houk Group).

I serve as an ad hoc reviewer for ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces.

Research Interests

  • Computational Mechanistic Chemistry: DFT studies of C–H activation, cross-coupling, and asymmetric catalysis
  • Organometallic & Polymer Chemistry: transition-metal catalyst synthesis; sustainable polymer upcycling
  • AI for Chemistry: machine learning for reaction prediction and catalyst design
  • Bioinorganic Chemistry: metal complexes targeting zinc finger proteins
